Services

Focused support for teams entering — or struggling with — the Japanese market.

IC Eight offers a small set of services designed to improve adoption, clarity, and trust. Each engagement adapts to what your product actually needs — not to a checklist or a translation quota.

1. Japanese UX Review

A clear, concise review of how your current product feels to Japanese users. We highlight where confidence drops, where misunderstandings happen, and where the experience creates friction — across UI, onboarding, help access, and feature discovery.

Quick, actionable insights without heavy implementation work.

2. Content Tone Rebuild

Rewriting key messaging so it works for Japanese decision-makers. This isn’t translation — it’s language strategy.

We redesign copy so benefits, risks, and intent land clearly in Japanese. Useful for landing pages, product pages, email flows, and any place where tone affects adoption.

3. Japanese Product Readiness Audit

A structured evaluation of your product’s readiness for Japan. We review onboarding, help content, tutorials, templates, release notes, terminology, and trust signals.

The result is a prioritized plan: what to fix now, what can wait, and what will actually shift adoption.

The audit is detailed but pragmatic — designed for teams that want clarity, not a 60-page report.

4. AI-Assisted Localization Direction

For teams already using GPT, DeepL, or internal LLM workflows. We define what can be automated, what cannot, and how to build Japanese output that doesn’t break UX.

This includes guardrails, prompt structures, terminology guidance, and quality thresholds tailored to your product.

5. Full Localization Package (EN → JA)

Translation done when it matters — as part of building a coherent Japanese experience. This package includes the UX perspective from above services and ensures Japanese output that feels written, intentional, and ready for adoption.

AI-generated drafts can be incorporated. DeepL-style output often requires full rewriting.

Not sure where to start?

The easiest entry point is a Japanese UX Review. You’ll see immediately where language is blocking adoption — and what the next steps should be.
