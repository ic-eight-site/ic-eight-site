Services

Practical support for building the Japanese language layer of your product — designed for clarity, trust, and adoption.

All IC Eight services start from one assumption: translation alone does not create a usable product. We diagnose what blocks Japanese adoption — and fix only what needs fixing.

Who This Is For

IC Eight works with teams that care about clarity — not just translation. Typical clients include:

  • Product teams releasing globally and offering early Japanese support.
  • EdTech platforms localizing onboarding, tests, and tutorials.
  • AI-first startups using GPT or DeepL but needing tone control and coherence.
  • Documentation and help teams focused on in-product guidance.
  • Product-led companies with Japanese users but no local team.
  • Growth and CS teams noticing churn from unclear language UX.

If you have Japanese users — or want to — IC Eight helps make the experience work.

1. Japanese UX Review

A clear, concise review of how your product feels to Japanese users. We identify friction: trust issues, confusing UI wording, missing help, broken onboarding, unclear feature discovery.

This service is diagnostic by design. You can implement the fixes internally, or ask IC Eight to handle specific items.

2. Japanese Product Readiness Audit

A structured evaluation of your product’s readiness for Japan. We review onboarding flows, help content, tutorials, templates, release notes, terminology, and overall language UX.

The output is a prioritized plan — what matters now, what can wait, and what will shift adoption. The audit stands alone; execution is optional.

3. Content Tone Rebuild

Some products need more than translation — they need a Japanese narrative that connects. We rebuild your key content (lead, CTA, headers, summaries) into persuasive, native-quality Japanese that feels written for Japanese users, not translated for them.

Optional: Tone notes or language rationale can be provided upon request.

4. AI Localization Guardrails

Guidance for teams using GPT or DeepL internally.
We define what can be automated, what cannot, and how to prevent Japanese output from breaking your UX.

IC Eight provides guardrails, fail conditions, quality criteria, and lightweight prompts.
You send the English source; we help your team produce native-feeling Japanese — with light review included where it matters.

It’s a one-time setup that gives your team confidence — with optional ongoing review for future releases.

5. Full Localization Package (EN → JA)

Translation only where it matters — as part of building a coherent Japanese experience. This package integrates the UX lens of the services above and delivers adoption-ready Japanese across UI, help, and product content.

We draw on the right approach for each context — whether it’s copywriting, translation, or UX-first rewriting — to make your product feel intuitive and trustworthy in Japanese.

AI-generated drafts can be used as input. Outputs like DeepL often require full rewriting.

Translation is just one tool — used only when it strengthens clarity and trust.
