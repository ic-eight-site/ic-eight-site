Why We Exist

I spent over 25 years as a translator — including work for companies like Sun Microsystems — and hated every moment I wasn’t allowed to ask:

  • Why does this need to be translated?
  • Will this make users act?

Translation assumes the source is right.
But most of the time, the source is the problem.

So I stopped translating.
Now I help teams fix the Japanese layer of their product — before it ever reaches a translator.

What We Do

  1. Audit your current UX in Japanese
    We'll show you what’s unclear, risky, or just off.
  2. Rebuild key messages with LLMs
    Instead of translating, we generate native-Japanese UX copy — guided by your intent.
  3. Give you a scalable workflow
    We set up reusable prompts and workflows so your team can keep moving fast.

Why It Works

Great UX starts before translation. By designing in Japanese — not just translating — you get faster feedback loops, fewer vendor handoffs, and content that actually performs.
