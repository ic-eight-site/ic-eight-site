Q: What if we don’t have a localization manager?
A: That’s totally fine — most early-stage teams don’t.
I can help you design a lightweight workflow that fits your current setup, so you don’t need to hire a full PM or adopt a complex TMS. If you already have someone handling vendors, I’ll make sure the process integrates smoothly with theirs

Q: Do you manage projects, too?
A: Not in the traditional “project-manager” sense.
Instead, I focus on making the workflow self-sustaining and maintaining tone, terminology, and consistency across all deliverables — which often means there’s less to manage in the first place.

Q: We’re planning multilingual localization. Can you work as part of a global team?
A: Yes. We often collaborate as the Japanese specialists within multilingual localization teams — working alongside English, Spanish, and French counterparts to keep tone and terminology aligned. If your other language partners already have established workflows, we can adapt easily to that structure, or help document the Japanese workflow as a reference model for other languages.

Q: Why don’t most companies hire freelancers directly instead of using vendors?
A: Usually not because of quality — but logistics.
Paying and managing multiple freelancers across different countries means handling separate contracts, NDAs, currencies, and invoices.
We simplify that: IC Eight operates as a registered business that can work directly with you, handle compliance and invoicing cleanly, and still keep the creative process transparent.
