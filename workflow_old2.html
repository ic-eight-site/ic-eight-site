Workflow

Localization that starts with reality, not with strings.

Japanese adoption rarely fails because of grammar. It fails because users can’t find help, don’t understand features, or don’t trust the interface. Our workflow is designed to fix that.

1. Understand the current state

Whether you already have Japanese text or none at all, we look at how the product feels today:

  • UI language and consistency
  • Onboarding flow
  • Help center and tutorials
  • Templates and sample content
  • Release notes and feature announcements

We identify friction points — where Japanese users hesitate, misunderstand, or drop off.

2. Define what matters first

Localization isn’t “translate everything.” It’s prioritizing what drives adoption:

  • Help pages
  • Tutorials and walkthroughs
  • Onboarding guidance
  • Language around new features

If a Japanese layer exists — NMT, GPT, DeepL, or mixed — we evaluate what can be salvaged and what requires rebuilding.

3. Rebuild the Japanese experience

This may involve:

  • Rewriting UI text
  • Translating or restructuring help pages
  • Creating Japanese versions of tutorials
  • Adapting templates and headers
  • Clarifying feature explanations
  • Filling gaps where English guidance is missing

We translate, rewrite, or restructure — whichever creates clarity and confidence.

4. Ensure coherence everywhere

Japanese should feel intentional, not stitched together. We align tone, terminology, and UX language across:

  • UI
  • Website
  • Help center
  • Templates
  • Emails
  • Onboarding flows

The goal is a product that feels consistent and trustworthy end-to-end.

5. Deliver fast, clean output

You get Japanese that feels written — not stitched together. Feedback cycles are short. Fixing AI-generated drafts is possible, but outputs like DeepL often require full rewriting.

6. Improve as you grow

Localization compounds. We stay available for new features, upcoming releases, and continuous refinement as your product expands.
